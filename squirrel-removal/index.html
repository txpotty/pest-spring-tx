Understanding Our Squirrel Removal Process

Our systematic approach to squirrel removal ensures thoroughness and effectiveness from start to finish. We don't just remove the squirrels; we address the root cause of the infestation and mitigate future risks.

Initial Inspection and Assessment

The first step in our squirrel removal process is a detailed inspection of your property. Our technicians will identify entry points, assess the extent of the infestation, locate nesting sites, and determine the species of squirrel involved. This assessment helps us develop a customized removal plan tailored to your specific situation.

Identifying Entry Points

Squirrels can enter structures through surprisingly small openings. Common access points include damaged roofing, soffit vents, chimneys, gaps in siding, foundation cracks, and openings around utility lines. Thoroughly inspecting the exterior and interior of your property is crucial for identifying all potential entry points.

Assessing the Extent of the Infestation

Understanding how many squirrels are present and where they are nesting is vital for effective removal. We look for signs such as droppings, gnaw marks, tracks, and disturbed insulation. This assessment informs our trapping strategy and the number of traps needed.

Humane Trapping and Removal

Once the assessment is complete, we implement the trapping phase. We utilize humane live traps to capture squirrels safely. These traps are strategically placed near known entry points and nesting areas.

Placement of Live Traps

Trap placement is key to successful trapping. We carefully select locations that are likely to be frequented by squirrels, such as near entry points, in attics, or on rooftops. The traps are baited with attractive food sources.

Monitoring and Relocation

We regularly monitor the traps to ensure squirrels are removed promptly after capture. Once a squirrel is captured, it is safely transported to a suitable location far from your property, following local regulations and best practices for wildlife relocation.

Exclusion and Prevention

Removing the squirrels is only part of the solution. To prevent future infestations, we focus on sealing entry points and implementing preventative measures.

Sealing Entry Points

Our technicians will repair and seal all identified entry points to prevent squirrels from regaining access to your property. We use durable materials designed to withstand squirrel gnawing and environmental factors.

Making Your Property Less Appealing

In addition to sealing entry points, we can offer recommendations to make your property less attractive to squirrels. This might include trimming tree branches away from your roofline, securing garbage cans, and removing potential food sources.

Signs You Might Need Squirrel Removal Services

Squirrels often give clear indications of their presence. Recognizing these signs early can help you address the problem before it escalates and causes significant damage. Being vigilant and knowing what to look for is an important first step in protecting your home from these persistent rodents. Ignoring the initial signs can lead to a more extensive and costly infestation down the line. Pay close attention to any unusual sounds or visible changes around your property, as these can be early warning signs of squirrel activity.

Scratching or Skittering Noises in Attic or Walls

One of the most common signs of a squirrel infestation is the presence of noises coming from your attic or inside your walls, particularly during the day. Squirrels are most active during the morning and late afternoon, so you are more likely to hear them during these times. The sounds can range from light scratching and rustling to more pronounced skittering and thumping as they move around. These noises are often indicative of squirrels nesting or foraging for food within the structure of your home. Distinguishing between squirrel sounds and those of other pests like rodents or raccoons can sometimes be challenging, but the timing and type of sounds often provide clues. Squirrels are generally more active during the day, unlike nocturnal animals. If you hear these sounds, it's a strong indicator that something is amiss and requires investigation.

Visible Damage to Exterior of Home

Squirrels have strong teeth and a natural urge to gnaw. They will chew on various materials to gain access to your home, sharpen their teeth, or gather nesting materials. Look for visible signs of damage on the exterior of your property, such as chewed fascia boards, soffit vents, window frames, or siding. They may also chew on roofing materials like shingles or flashing. This damage not only creates entry points for squirrels but can also compromise the structural integrity of your home and leave it vulnerable to water damage and other pests. Inspecting your roofline and eaves regularly can help you spot these signs early. Sometimes, the damage might be high up and less obvious from ground level, requiring a closer look or the use of binoculars. Addressing this damage promptly is crucial for both squirrel prevention and overall home maintenance.

Chewed Electrical Wiring

This is one of the most dangerous consequences of a squirrel infestation. Squirrels are known to gnaw on electrical wiring, often because they are attracted to the insulation material. This can create significant fire hazards, potentially leading to devastating house fires. If you notice flickering lights, power surges, or detect a burning smell, it could be a sign of damaged wiring caused by squirrels. Prompt action is necessary to mitigate this serious risk. Electrical damage caused by squirrels requires the expertise of a qualified electrician in addition to squirrel removal professionals. The risk of electrocution is also present when dealing with live wires, making direct interaction with the infestation highly discouraged. Safety should always be the top priority.

Damaged or Contaminated Insulation

Squirrels often build their nests in attics, using insulation as a primary nesting material. They will tear and compact insulation, reducing its effectiveness and leading to energy loss. Squirrel droppings and urine can also contaminate insulation, creating unpleasant odors and potential health hazards. The presence of squirrel nests and contaminated insulation is a clear indication of an ongoing infestation that requires professional attention. Damaged insulation can lead to higher heating and cooling bills as your HVAC system works harder to maintain a comfortable temperature. Furthermore, squirrel waste can attract other pests and create an unsanitary environment. Proper cleanup and decontamination of infested areas are essential after the squirrels have been removed. This often involves removing the damaged insulation and replacing it with new material.

Squirrels Visible on Property

Seeing squirrels frequently on or around your property, especially near your home’s roofline or eaves, can be a sign that they are attempting to gain entry or have already established a presence. While it's normal to see squirrels in your yard occasionally, persistent sightings and observed attempts to access your home are concerning. Pay attention to their behavior; if they are seen scurrying up walls, attempting to access vents, or lingering near potential entry points, it's a strong indication of an issue. The presence of young squirrels, typically observed during nesting season (spring and late summer), can also indicate a breeding population on your property. Seeing multiple squirrels regularly implies a more significant infestation than a lone scout. Observing them carrying nesting materials is another clear sign that they are establishing a home within your structure. Early detection based on visual cues can lead to faster and easier resolution of the problem.

Unusual Odors

Squirrel infestations can produce distinct, unpleasant odors. The accumulation of droppings and urine in confined spaces like attics can create strong ammonia-like smells. Deceased squirrels within walls or attics can also cause foul odors as they decompose. These odors can permeate throughout your home and are a significant indicator of a hidden wildlife problem. These smells can be a health concern, particularly for individuals with respiratory sensitivities. The longer the infestation goes untreated, the more intense and pervasive the odors can become. Identifying the source of the odor is crucial for effective remediation. Sometimes, the smell might not be immediately obvious but becomes noticeable when temperatures rise. Trust your nose; persistent unusual odors are a red flag that should be addressed. Proper ventilation and professional cleaning are often necessary to eliminate odors after removal.

Droppings and Urine Stains

Finding squirrel droppings or urine stains in your attic, garage, or other areas of your home is a definitive sign of an infestation. Squirrel droppings are typically elongated and pellet-shaped, similar to those of rats but often slightly larger. Urine stains can appear as yellowish or brownish marks on insulation or other surfaces. These waste products not only create unsanitary conditions but can also pose health risks. Inhaling airborne particles from droppings can lead to respiratory issues. The presence of fresh droppings indicates an active infestation. The location and quantity of droppings can provide clues about the squirrels' movements and nesting areas. Proper handling and cleanup of wildlife waste require caution due to potential pathogens. It's advisable to wear protective gear when inspecting areas with droppings. Professional cleanup services are recommended to ensure thorough sanitization of contaminated areas.

Damage to Stored Items

Squirrels may access storage areas like attics or garages and cause damage to stored items. They might chew on boxes, furniture, or other belongings to gather nesting material or simply out of curiosity. Damaged insulation as mentioned earlier is a prime example of this. Discovering unexplained damage to items stored in these areas could point to squirrel activity. The type of damage, such as gnaw marks on wood or shredded fabric, can help identify the culprit. This damage can be costly to repair or replace. Securely storing valuables in airtight containers can help protect them from wildlife damage. Inspecting stored items periodically can help detect issues before they become extensive. Squirrels are opportunistic and will take advantage of accessible spaces to build their nests, including storage areas. Therefore, keeping these areas tidy and decluttered can also deter infestations.

Bird Feeder or Garden Damage

While not directly related to an indoor infestation, persistent raiding of bird feeders or damage to your garden can be an indicator of a large squirrel population in your area that may eventually attempt to or already have entered your home. Squirrels are notorious for their ability to access bird feeders and consume large quantities of birdseed. They may also dig up plants or eat fruits and vegetables in your garden. Increased activity in these areas could signal a higher likelihood of indoor problems. Implementing squirrel-proof measures for bird feeders and gardens can help mitigate these exterior issues and potentially reduce attraction to your property. While outdoor squirrel activity is common, excessive behavior could indicate an overpopulation or a lack of natural food sources, leading them to seek alternatives, including your home. Understanding and managing outdoor squirrel activity can contribute to preventing indoor problems. If you notice an unusual increase in squirrels in your yard, it's wise to be extra vigilant about potential entry points into your home. This includes checking for gaps in the foundation or around the roofline.

Additionally, improper removal methods can cause unnecessary stress and harm to the animal and may not effectively solve the problem. Wildlife control requires specific knowledge, training, and equipment to be done safely and humanely. Professional pest control technicians are equipped to handle wildlife encounters safely and effectively, minimizing risks to both the animals and the residents. DIY methods can sometimes exacerbate the problem, driving squirrels deeper into your home or causing them to create new entry points. The humane treatment of wildlife is also a consideration, and professionals are trained in ethical removal practices. Avoiding direct contact with squirrels protects you from potential bites or scratches and the risk of zoonotic diseases. Professional intervention ensures the job is done correctly the first time, preventing prolonged issues. Attempting self-removal could potentially lead to legal issues if not done in accordance with local laws.
